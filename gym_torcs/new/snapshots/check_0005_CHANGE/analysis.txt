Analysis:
The car finishes laps consistently but at ~2:40-3:50 (8000-11000 steps), far from the 1:30 target. Critically, the finish bonus `120000/time_step` REWARDS slow laps less but the per-step on-track reward accumulates over MORE steps, so a 3:51 lap (ep_000734.28, 11572 steps, 861k reward) earns MORE total reward than a 2:45 lap (ep_000800.28, 7927 steps, 593k). This directly inverts the operator's goal — surviving longer pays more than going faster. The reward function must penalize step count / time so fewer steps to cover distance wins.

Action:
Rewrote the reward so TIME is the dominant signal: (1) added a per-step time penalty (-3.0 each step) so every step alive costs reward — only forward progress justifies it, killing the "slow lap = more accumulated reward" exploit; (2) raised the finish bonus time coefficient and made it scale steeply with lap time so a sub-1:30 lap dwarfs a 3:00 lap; (3) increased speed reward weight and raised SPEED_TARGET to push pace harder. Loaded the fastest finishing branch (ep_000800.28, 2:45) to continue from a fast policy rather than a slow-but-long one.
